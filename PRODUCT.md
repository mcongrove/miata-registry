# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

**Primary:** Owners and enthusiasts of limited edition Mazda Miatas — browsing the registry, researching editions, claiming or registering cars, and reading news/history.

**Secondary:** Moderators who review owner, car, and ownership submissions.

Typical context: unhurried research at a desk or on mobile; users care about accuracy, provenance, and whether a car is verified. Many visits are lookup-driven (VIN, edition, year, rarity), not casual scrolling.

## Product Purpose

Miata Registry is the community-maintained record of limited edition Miatas: who owns them, what makes each edition distinct, and how cars change hands over time. Success means trustworthy data, visible recent community activity, and workflows (claim, register, moderate) that feel dependable rather than flashy.

## Positioning

The authoritative registry for limited edition Miatas — a club archive with real verification, not a generic classifieds or spec-sheet site.

## Brand Personality

Calm, archival, enthusiast-confident. Understated and legible. Reads like a serious hobbyist institution, not a growth startup. Voice is plain and respectful of the cars and the people who keep them.

**Three words:** archival, trustworthy, understated.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS / AI-tool marketing pages (purple gradients, glassmorphism, neon accents, floating orbs).
- Crypto-dashboard dark mode or "premium automotive" black-and-gold clichés.
- Busy auction-listing UIs with badge spam and aggressive CTAs.
- Over-rounded, bubbly "friendly app" aesthetics that undermine credibility.
- Hype copy ("revolutionary", "supercharge", "unlock") — the cars are the story.

## Design Principles

- **Trust through restraint.** Let photography, registry data, and verification status carry weight; UI stays out of the way.
- **Familiar product patterns.** Tables, filters, forms, and modals should feel like tools people already know how to use.
- **Heritage over hype.** The site should feel like a long-running club record, not a launch landing page — even on the home screen.
- **Palette discipline.** The `brg` green family is the brand; don't decorate with extra accent colors.
- **Terminology precision.** Say *limited edition* and *edition* in user-facing copy; avoid "special edition" and "model" for registry cars.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA contrast on text and controls. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` for non-essential animation (hero fades, arrow nudges, pulse skeletons). Form fields use visible focus borders (`focus:border-brg-mid`). Clerk handles auth UI; keep custom surfaces consistent with our field and button patterns.
